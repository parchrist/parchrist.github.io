How I Got Into Programming

At heart am a data nerd with an interest in artificial intelligence and machine learning. I am currently pursuing a Master's in Applied Artificial Intelligence at the University of San Diego. A majority my side quests is spent on working on things like Quantatative analysis, and computer vision. When I'm not coding, I enjoy working on my compute homeLab or I am skiing!

My journey into programming started during my studies at ASU during my political science degree, where I found myself drawn to data analysis and starting to use R for general stats and T-tests. I disliked the fact that we where taught in excel for data analysis and wanted something executable. So I picked up R for a little bit, but in no way do I consider myself good at R.

I became fascinated with high-frequency trading, which sparked a deeper interest in algorithms, automation, and the fast-paced world of tech in general. This curiosity led me down the rabbit hole of python, as I realized that the begining statistical analysis and discovery would be done using python, then the actual trading would be done in C++.

From political science analysis to high-frequency trading and database design, programming has shown me that the goal is to fail, and while doing so, you steadily improve on your incompetencies to become better and more efficent.
